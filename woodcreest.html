What is wood veneer?

Wood Veneers are thin slices of wood's upper layer and are typically less than 3mm thick and are attached to panels made of wood or particle board to form flat panels.

What is the difference between laminate and Veneer?

Decorative Wood Veneer is actually a thin sheet of real hardwood timber which is glued onto plywood surfaces for visual enhancement, whereas laminate, also known as sun mica in India is an artificial product made by pressing together man-made paper and plastic resin.

Why to use Wood Veneer?
